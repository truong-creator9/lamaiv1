## Before Starting — Understanding What a Money Model Is

---

> **What is a Money Model?**
>
> Imagine you go to rent a car. The listed price is $19/day.
> But when you check out, the bill is $100/day.
>
> Why? Because the staff asked you one question at a time:
> - "Would you like a bigger car?" → Car upgrade
> - "Would you like a late return?" → Late fee
> - "Would you like insurance?" → Insurance fee
> - "Would you like to prepay for gas?" → Prepaid gas
>
> No one forced you. But each well-timed question → the customer voluntarily bought more.
> That is a **Money Model** — a sequence of offers arranged in the right order.
>
> **A business without a Money Model** = only gets $19.
> **A business with a Money Model** = collects $100 from the same customer.

---

> **4 offer types in a complete Money Model:**
>
> **1. ATTRACTION — Turn strangers into customers**
> Free or very low-priced to bring people in. 6 types:
> - **Win Your Money Back:** Deposit + refund if goal is achieved
>   *"Join the 21-day challenge, complete all tasks — get 100% deposit back"*
> - **Giveaway:** Raffle a big prize; non-winners get to buy at a discount
>   *"Win 1 free year; everyone else gets 50% off"*
> - **Decoy Offer:** Advertise a free/cheap offer; show the real offer when they ask
>   *"Download free checklist → when they message → introduce the full course"*
> - **Buy X Get Y Free:** Buy one thing, get something else free
>   *"Buy 6-month course, get another 6 months free"*
> - **Pay Less Now or Pay More Later:** Pay less now + bonus, or pay more later
>   *"Register today for 500k, or register later for 800k"*
> - **Free Goodwill:** Give real value away completely free — no strings attached
>   *"Free tutorial video, no email required"*
>
> **2. UPSELL — Increase value right after they buy**
> Offer something additional right when the customer just purchased — this is when they're most likely to buy again. 4 types:
> - **Classic Upsell:** Offer a solution to the next problem that appears after buying
>   *"You just bought the course — want to add 1-on-1 coaching to make sure you succeed?"*
> - **Menu Upsell:** Show them what they DON'T need first, then suggest what they actually need
>   *"You don't need that package — you need this one more"*
> - **Anchor Upsell:** Show the most expensive offer first, then the cheaper one looks like a deal
>   *"VIP package at 50M — or this one is enough at just 10M"*
> - **Rollover Upsell:** Apply what they already paid toward a new, higher-priced offer
>   *"You paid 2M for the challenge — I'll apply that to the 12M annual package, just 10M more"*
>
> **3. DOWNSELL — Turn "no" into "yes"**
> When a customer declines the main offer — don't let them leave empty-handed. 3 types:
> - **Payment Plan:** Break up the payment — same price but paid in installments
>   *"Can't pay 12M all at once? Pay 1M/month for 12 months"*
> - **Trial With Penalty:** Free trial, but requires a deposit — don't complete and lose the deposit
>   *"Try free for 7 days, leave a card — participate fully and nothing is charged"*
> - **Feature Downsell:** Remove features, reduce price — ask "would this work for you?"
>   *"No support group needed? Just 297k instead of 497k"*
>
> **4. CONTINUITY — Collect money regularly every month/year**
> Offers where customers pay on a recurring basis — creates the most stable cash flow. 3 types:
> - **Continuity Bonus:** Give something of high value if they sign up today
>   *"Sign up for membership today — get a free 2M course as a bonus"*
> - **Continuity Discount:** Give free time if they sign up today
>   *"Sign up for 1 year, get 2 extra months free"*
> - **Waived Fee:** Setup fee if buying monthly, free setup if committing annually
>   *"Monthly: 1M/month + 3M setup fee. Annual commitment: 1M/month, free setup"*
>
> **Goal:** Use all 4 types = each customer can be worth 3-10x more
> compared to only selling to them once.
